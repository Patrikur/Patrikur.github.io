This game is an abbreviated version of the original show airing on CBS in the USA and several other countries around the world in different game versions. This version is specially developed for a browser-based version.

Before playing the game

The game master has to make sure to have all answers on the screen or a sheet of paper to reveal the right answers or note a strike for every wrong answer. Teams should consist of the same amount of players for each team.

Game rules

Teams

Teams are made up of family members, friends or random people. Make sure to chose a nice team name. These two teams are made up of an equal number of team members and play against each other in every game. Every team must provide an order in which they are playing and starting each round with, e. g. player one starts round one, player two starts round two and so on.

The Questions

The answers to the questions are unique and represent a total percentage of the top given answers according to each round. The game starts with round one, where the top six answers are asked for. Contestants are challenged to come up with the correct answers to each question, which are placed on the game board and revealed as teams provide them. The questions can either be fact-based or given by a survey among interviewed persons.

Playing the Main Game

The main game starts with one team member from each team coming to the virtual podium and facing off on the first question. The contestant whose team is chosen by the random generator gets to provide the first answer. If that answer is the No. 1 response, his or her team gets control of the question. If not, the opposing contestant gets to try and provide a higher-ranking response to earn control for his or her family.
The team that wins control of the question then provides more responses, one at a time by each team member following the order the team has given before. They are not permitted to consult with one another during this part of the game. If a given answer is not under the top answers, the team gets a strike.

If the team can guess all of the top answers on the board before getting three strikes, they win the round.

If a team ends up with three strikes, control of the round goes to the opposing team. That team then has one chance to come up with one of the remaining responses on the board to win the round — if they fail, the other team gets the points.

Generally speaking, four main rounds are played in each game. But there can be added as many rounds as the host inserts into the questions data file.

Point Values

The point values assigned to each answer come from the percentage within the top given answers. The first round brings the single value of the points, the second double amount and so on. The questions are getting harder each round.

Controls

Starting the Game

You start the game by pressing the “S” on your keyboard.
Pressing “E” will enable the title melody of the German version called “Familien Duell”.
Pressing “M” brought you to this manual site.

In the game


After making sure that the teams are ready to play, you can press “Q” to reveal the question for the current round.
If the right answer is given, the game master presses the button for the position of the correct answer in the ranking.
If the answer is wrong, the game master presses “X” for a strike. As soon as a team has three strikes the light for the team turn switches automatically over to the other team, giving them the opportunity to steal the current rounds points with one right answer. If a right answer is given the game master presses the respective number or in case of a wrong answer “X” for a strike. In both cases, the rounds point will be added to the team’s overall points and the round is over. Not revealed answers can be revealed by pressing the respective number, points will not be added to the current rounds points and/or the team’s overall points. Pressing “Enter” will start the next round.
If the last round is played, pressing enter will bring the game to the final screen where the winning team is revealed.
